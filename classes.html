Monday

10:00-11:00 Qigong. Threading the Pearl with Nine Bends

With Eva - open to all. Threading the Pearl with Nine Bends Tai Xu, Dragons, Tigers and other animals. Focusing on wrists, elbows, shoulders, neck, ribcage, waist, hips, knees and ankles.

14:00-15:00 Taiji (Laojia) exercises

With Eva - open to all. Taiji exercises for coordination, cognition, balance and effortless movement in everyday life. This is an excellent introduction to learning Chen Taijiquan without having to remember the sequence of the moves which can be difficult to master during the first few months. It is also invaluable for students learning the form as it deepens their understanding of how to move the body functionally, efficiently and effortlessly, difficult to achieve when focusing on learning and remembering the moves.

Tuesday

10:00-11:00 Daoist Health Qigong & Tai Xu

With Eva - open to all Daoist Health Exercises are gentle spiralling exercises that involve the whole body with the focus on the dantian and the spine. Including ten exercises: Swimming Smiling Dragon, Vital Energy, Taiji Diagram, Restoring Spring, Phoenix Spreads Wings, Heavenly and Earthly Circles, Peng Flying etc. Also Tai Xu - eight exercises for strengthening the body and promoting internal energy. They also stretch the muscles allowing the nerves to floss and glide from the cervical spine all the way to the finger tips.

11:30-12:30 Xinjia Exercises

With Karel - some experience of taiji preferable. Xinjia exercises are excellent for coordination and cognition.

20:00-21:00 Yiquan

With Karel - open to all. Standing, almost movement (Moli), slow movements (Shili) and fast movements (Fali) - using the intent to strengthen to pathways in the brain to improve the coordination and strength of the muscles in the body. See our website www.yiquan.org.uk for more information.

Wednesday

10:00-11:00 Chen Taijiquan Laojia

With Eva - for students who know the form up to third Jin Gang Pounds Mortar. Flowing Sequence, Chan Si Gong/silk reeling exercises and movements from the first section of the form.

Saturday

9:00-10:00 Baguazhang

FULL. With Eva. Baguazhang - Eight Dragon Palms is a basic form of Bagua. The movements are based on nimble footwork and flexible and spiralling movements of the whole body. Though it is more physical demanding than taiji and qigong, it can be enjoyable to master a challenging martial art.

Sunday

9:00-10:00 Qigong for Health and Balance

With Eva - open to all We shall focus on harmonising the body and the mind through various types of Qigong, improving the balance of the body (deteriorating in our twenties), and the strength of the body (deteriorating in our thirties). Animal Qigong: Bear and Eagle, Snake and Crane, Turtle and Peng, Tiger and Dragon; and also Yin Yang Qigong (yijinjing) - muscle/tendon strengthening exercises as taught by Grandmaster Chen Xiaowang, and Tai Xu - eight postures for internal strength.
